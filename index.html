GDP vs GNP

Sort each scenario for the U.S. into the correct bin. Then compute GDP, GNP, and NFIA. Focus on the rule: GDP = where production occurs; GNP = who earns the income.

How this works
  • GDP: production within U.S. borders (location-based).
  • GNP: production by U.S. residents anywhere + NFIA (ownership/residency-based).
  • Identity: GNP = GDP + NFIA, where NFIA is net factor income from abroad.

Tip: Some cards are not production (used goods, transfers, financial transactions). Those go in Neither.
