GDP vs GNP

Sort each card for the U.S. into the correct bucket. GDP is about where production occurs. GNP is about who earns the income.

How this works
  • GDP: value of final goods/services produced inside the U.S.
  • GNP: GDP + NFIA, where NFIA is net factor income from abroad
  • NFIA: income received from abroad by U.S. factors minus income paid to foreign factors

In this lab, factor payments to foreign residents go in Neither (they reduce GNP through NFIA).
